Credit Card Dataset

Exploratory Data Analysis

  • Here's a snapshot of what the data looks like
  • 10127 rows and 21 columns
  • Fairly clean data - no NaNs or duplicate entries
  • Attrited Customer indicates those who cancelled their credit cards
  • Existing Customer are those who continued their credit card service
  • Dataset is skewed towards customer retention with 84% of customers NOT cancelling their credit cards
  • Heatmap showing positive and negative correlations within the data that we will explore with scatter plots
  • Positive correlation between how long the customer has had the credit card and the customer's age
  • Slight negative correlation between the customer's age and how many dependents the customer has
  • Negative correlation between the customer's credit limit and average utilization ratio
  • No surpise here - having a lower credit limit generally indicates a lower credit limit
  • Females tend to have lower credit limits than men in the same income category
  • Interesting - it seems that the lower the customer's income is the more likey the customer is to churn
  • Very surprsing find - customers with graduate degrees are more significantly more likely to churn than those with other levels of education
  • Females narrowly beat out males for being more likely to churn
  • Most churning card category is the entry level "blue" credit card
  • Prediction - naive bayes classifier
  • Ratio of 80% - 20% for train and test data
  • Consistently acheiving 80% accuarcy rate
